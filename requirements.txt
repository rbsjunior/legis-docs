## Organizational Structure

```
Department
├── Administrations  (each headed by a Sr. Deputy Director)
│   └── Bureaus → Divisions → Sections
└── Standalone Bureaus / Divisions  (no Sr. Deputy Director above them)
```

**Top-level Executives:** Director, COO, CAO, Health Services Director, Chief Medical Executive

## User Roles

| Role | Code | Division | Purpose |
|------|------|----------|---------|
| Administrator | `ADMIN` | Legislative Affairs | System administration and emergency overrides |
| Legislative Affairs Initiator | `LAI` | Legislative Affairs | Create and initiate bill analyses |
| Administrative Point of Contact | `APOC` | Legislative Affairs | Coordinate bill analysis and review per bill |
| Legislative Affairs Contact | `LA_CONTACT` | Legislative Affairs | First-level approval |
| Subject Matter Expert | `SME` | Any Bureau / Division / Administration | Provide expert analysis and review |
| Senior Deputy Director | `SENIOR_DEPUTY` | Administration | Administration-level approval and/or SME feedback |
| Chief Operating Officer | `COO` | Executive | Required approver on all analyses |
| Director | `DIRECTOR` | Executive | Final approval authority — required on all analyses |
| CAO | `CAO` | Executive | Executive approval — required in some cases |
| Health Services Director | `HSD` | Executive | Executive approval — required in some cases |
| Chief Medical Executive | `CME` | Executive | Executive approval — required in some cases |
| Proxy Approver | `PROXY_APPROVER` | Any | Temporary approval authority in absence of primary approver |

**Legislative Affairs division roles** (`ADMIN`, `LAI`, `APOC`, `LA_CONTACT`) have full read and edit access to all bill records. All other roles have scoped access limited to bills they are assigned to (to be refined).

**Multi-role support:** A user may hold multiple roles simultaneously (e.g. `LAI` + `APOC`). Access rights are the union of all held roles.

## Approval Path Logic

All analyses require **COO** and **Director** approval. `CAO`, `HSD`, and `CME` are required in some cases (configurable per bill). All approver roles may provide substantive feedback in addition to their approval decision.

| Scenario | Approval Path |
|---|---|
| SME in an Administration | SME → Sr. Deputy Director → COO → Director |
| SME in a standalone Bureau or Division | SME → COO → Director |
| Sr. Deputy acts as SME (no separate SME review) | Sr. Deputy Director → COO → Director |

- A bill may involve **zero or more Administrations** and **zero or more standalone Bureaus/Divisions**.
- Multiple Administration paths run **in parallel**, converging at COO.

```
[Admin A SMEs]  → Sr. Deputy A ─┐
[Admin B — Sr. Deputy only] ────┼──→ COO → Director
[Standalone Bureau SMEs] ───────┘
```

## Full Workflow

**START — Trigger:** Legislative Proposal needs MDHHS analysis.

### Step 1 — LAI Creates and Submits Analysis Record
1. **LAI** creates an analysis record and assigns: APOC, LA Contact.
2. **LAI** submits the analysis record.
3. *(Auto-email)* Notifies: APOC, LA Contact.

### Step 2 — APOC Facilitates Administration Review
4. **APOC** accesses record and views details.
5. **APOC** assigns SMEs and Senior Deputies for the record.
6. **APOC** facilitates administration review and content contribution.
7. **APOC** ensures bill analysis is agreed on by administration SMEs.
8. **APOC** creates the approval path, assigning SMEs and Senior Deputies per parallel path.
9. *(Auto-email)* Notifies: APOC, LAI.

### Step 3 — Administration Review Completion & LAI Review
10. Administration review confirmed complete.
11. **APOC** triggers automated message to LAI.
12. *(Auto-email)* Notifies: APOC, LAI.
13. **LAI** reviews the analysis.
14. Executive Approvers and Proxies added to approval path.

### Step 4 — LAI Approval & Executive Approval Chain
15. **LAI** approves the analysis.
16. *(Auto-email)* Sends routing sheet to Legislative Affairs Manager.
17. **[PA]** **COO** reviews and approves.
18. **[PA]** **MDHHS Director** approves.

### Step 5 — Final Notification & Artifact Distribution
19. *(Auto-email)* Sends to **LAI**: Bill #, Sponsor, Topic, Recommended Position, link to record.
20. **LAI** clicks **"Email Artifacts"** button.
21. *(Auto-email)* Sends: PDF bill analysis, PDF routing sheet, Bill Analysis details.

**FINISH**

*[PA] = action can be completed by a Proxy Approver*

## Bill Analysis Record

*Source: OHS Bill Analysis/Enrolled Bill Analysis Template (doc-page-1 through doc-page-4)*

**One analysis per bill package.** A package contains one or more companion bills that share a single analysis record — the analysis content (Sections 2–13), workflow, and approval chain apply to the package as a whole. The Related Bill Numbers field cross-references other LEGIS records (e.g. prior-session bills or related analyses), and is separate from the bills within this package.

### Editing and Collaboration Rules

- **Section 1** locked after creation — LAI/APOC only.
- **Sections 2–12** editable by assigned stakeholders in fields relevant to their role.
- **Collaborative fields** — multiple stakeholders may contribute to the same text area; contributions are attributed (who/when); APOC or LAI consolidates.
- **Concurrent editing** — simultaneous updates permitted; last write wins.
- **Approval invalidation** — any edit after an approval is expressed automatically voids that approval; approver must re-approve.
- **Executive lock** — once LAI submits for executive approval, record is read-only for all except `COO`, `DIRECTOR`, `CAO`, `HSD`, `CME`.
- Audit trail captures all edits by all users with timestamps.

### Previous Department Review Reference

When a bill that was previously reviewed by the Department returns in a new or amended form, the new analysis record may be linked to the prior review. This provides continuity of institutional knowledge and avoids duplicating work already done.

**Linking:**
- A new bill analysis record may reference one prior LEGIS bill analysis record (the "previous review").
- The link is set at creation time or any point during drafting by the LAI.
- The referenced record does not need to be for the same bill number — it may point to a prior-session version, a predecessor bill, or a substantially similar bill.

**Clone from previous review:**
- LAI may trigger a "Copy from previous review" action that pre-populates selected sections of the new record with content from the referenced prior review.
- Sections eligible for cloning (LAI selects which to copy): Summary, Intent of Legislation, Lead Agency Position, Position Details, Suggested Changes, Fiscal Impact, Background, Stakeholder Positions.
- Cloned content is treated as a starting draft — fully editable; the copy action does not lock or link the fields.
- Section 1 (Header) and all system/workflow fields are never cloned.
- The clone action can only be performed once per record; a confirmation prompt is shown before overwriting any existing content.

**Review history comparison:**
- On any bill analysis record that has a previous review linked, a "View Prior Review" panel or link is available to all users with record access.
- The panel shows the prior record's key fields (Bill Number, Topic, Lead Agency Position, Summary, date of review) side by side with the current record for quick reference.
- Full navigation to the prior record is available via a link.
- If the prior record itself has a previous review linked, the full chain is traversable (each record links to its predecessor), enabling a complete history of Department reviews for a recurring bill.

### Bill Document Attachments

Each bill analysis record supports one or more attached bill documents. PDF is the most common format; multiple documents may be attached to accommodate amendments, substitutes, or companion bills.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Primary document | Typically one PDF; displayed prominently on the record |
| Additional documents | Flexible — any number of supplementary files allowed |
| Accepted formats | PDF (primary), HTML, plain text, Markdown, XML |
| Upload method | File upload (stored in Railway Storage) and/or public URL — both optional, but at least one is required per document entry |
| Public URL | Each document entry has an optional public URL field (e.g. Michigan Legislature website link); stored alongside the uploaded file |
| Storage | Railway Storage (S3-compatible) via AWS SDK v3 for uploaded files |
| Access | Any user with access to the bill record can view attached documents |
| Management | LAI and APOC may add or remove documents at any workflow stage prior to executive lock |

**Document list display:** Each attached document shows label/filename, upload date, uploader name, and links for download (if file uploaded) and/or open URL (if URL provided).

**Bill draft comparison:** Documents are passed to a third-party comparison tool by URL. When two or more documents are present on the record, the system constructs a comparison link using their public URLs — either the user-supplied public URL or the Railway Storage download URL for uploaded files. The comparison tool is loaded in-app (e.g. new tab or embedded iframe — integration details TBD).

**AI-assisted comparison *(low priority / future)*:** A future evolution will use a generative AI tool to compare two versions of a bill and produce a plain-language synopsis of the changes (e.g. "Section 3 removes the appropriation; Section 7 adds a new reporting requirement"). This will be surfaced as an optional "Summarize Changes" action alongside the existing comparison link. Implementation details TBD — depends on third-party comparison tool maturity and LLM API selection.

### Mid-Workflow Bill Changes

If the bill is amended or substituted while department analysis is in progress, the LAI must be able to record the change without restarting the workflow.

**LAI actions when a bill changes:**
1. Upload the updated bill document (attached to the record alongside prior versions — old versions are retained for audit purposes, not deleted).
2. Enter a required comment describing what changed and why (e.g. "Substitute bill introduced 5/20 — Section 3 revised to remove appropriation language").

**System behavior on bill change:**
- The new document becomes the current primary bill document; prior versions are demoted and remain visible in the document history.
- The comment is recorded as a **bill change notice** — displayed prominently on the record and visible to all users with record access.
- All active approvals (any `APPROVED` decisions not yet `VOIDED`) are automatically voided, requiring re-approval, consistent with the existing approval invalidation rule.
- *(Auto-email — TBD)* Notify APOC, assigned SMEs, and Sr. Deputies that the bill has changed and approvals have been reset.

**Constraints:**
- Only LAI may record a bill change notice.
- Available at any workflow stage up to and including `LAI_REVIEW`. Not available once status reaches `EXECUTIVE_REVIEW` or later — at that point the LAI must contact an ADMIN for an override.
- Comment is required; upload of a new document is strongly recommended but not enforced (edge case: bill text changes but document is not yet available).
